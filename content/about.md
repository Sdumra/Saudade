why_built: "<p>Saudade exist because graduate school carries a weight nobody talks about, not loudly anyway. The silent struggles, the slow unraveling, the gap between who you thought you would be here and who you actually are, and the quiet journey back towards that version of yourself.</p><p>There is a feeling this life gives you that you cannot name. You carry it everywhere, in the lab, in seminars, on the drive home at night, and you never quite find the word for it. That is exactly why I chose “Saudade”. Because sometimes the most honest thing you can do is name the unnamed. A space we are building together where these stories can finally be expressed, and where even one sentence might move something in someone who needed it most.</p>"
about_me: "<p>I am a first generation woman who left a very small world to be here. Nobody in my family had done this before. I did not know the unwritten rules, I did not know who to ask, and most days I did not know if I even belonged. I am not building this as a women only space because these silent struggles in grad school does not discriminate. But that was my road in, and I am not going to hide it.</p><p>What took me the longest to understand is that wisdom is not knowing everything. It is sitting with not knowing and choosing to stay anyway. You will not figure it out on day one. Honestly you will not fully figure it out on the last day either. And somewhere in accepting that, the journey starts to mean something.</p><p>I remember the nights that felt endless. The car rides where you realize you have been quiet for too long. The slow distance that grows first between you and the people you love, and then between you and yourself. The voice that tells you you are not smart enough, not prepared enough, just not enough. I know that voice. I think most of us do.</p>"
hope: "It was never supposed to be this lonely. That is the truth nobody puts in the brochure. So I built this. Not because I had everything figured out but because I was tired of feeling like the only one. Your story does not have to be dramatic or resolved or even make sense yet. If you have ever felt the weight of this road, you belong here. If you have wanted to quit, you belong here. If some days you still do, you especially belong here. This is the warm hug I needed when I had no one to call at 2am. I hope it feels that way for you too."
